FAQ - Moralenet Frequently Asked Questions




This section contains a wealth of information, related to Troubleshootingand Morale-Net. If you cannot find an answer to your question, make sure to contact us.

General questions

Force Restart

A force restart can help to fix many minor glitches on Phone, including this WiFi issue. So, don't hesitate to give it a try. To force restart iPhone 8 and later:Simply press and release the volume up button quickly, press and release the volume down button quickly, then press and hold the side button until Apple logo shows up. To force restart iPhone7 or 7 plus:Press and hold the volume down button and power button at the same time unti you see the Apple logo. To force restart iPhone 6s and earlier:Press and hold the home button and power button simultaneously unti Apple logo appears on the screen. To force restart Android:press and hold the power button for at least 20 seconds. If Android isn't responding, this will (usually) force your device to reboot manually.

Forget and Re-join WiFi Network

In our experience, any WiFi network connection problems can be resolved by forgetting and rejoining the WiFi network. iPhone:Firstly, click on Settings >WiFi. In the wireless LAN interface, click the "i" icon behind the WiFi name. Then click "Forget This Network". In the pop-up of "Forget WiFi Network" interface, click to "Forget". Go back to the WiFi network interface, re-click the name of the WiFi network you want to connect to, and then enter the correct WiFi password to connect. Android:From the home screen, choose Settings. In the settings menu, choose Wi-Fi. Press and hold the Wi-Fi network to be removed, and then choose Forget.

Renew the Lease

iPhone:If forget the WiFi network doesn't solve the problem, you can try to renew the DHCP lease of your network and obtain a new IP address for your iPhone. To renew the lease of your network, follow these steps: Open the Settings app and go to WiFi, then tap on the "i" icon next to the Wi-Fi network. Scroll down to locate the "Renew Lease" option and tap on it. Tap on "Renew Lease" again on the popup to confirm the action. Android:The way to do this is by forcing the phone to “forget” the current Wi-Fi network to which it is connected. Once done, the next time the phone connects to the wifi network, it will automatically receive a new IP address.

Disable VPN from Settings

The WiFi connection on Phone may be unstable when VPN feature is enabled. Therefore, you may need to turn off this feature to see if it is woking to salve this WiFi disconnects when Phone is locked problem. iPhone:On your iPhone, go to Settings >General >VPN and toggle off the VPN if it is activated. Android:From your apps list, select Settings. Select Connections. Scroll down to More Connection Settings. Select VPN. Tap on the blue settings cog next to the VPN name. Select Delete VPN profile.

Turn Off Wi-Fi Assist

With Wi-Fi Assist, the Phone will turn on the cellular data when the connected Wi-Fi network is poor or there is no available Wi-Fi network. And this may be the cause of WiFi disconnects when Phone is locked or in sleep mode. So, you can turn off the Wi-Fi Assist. iPhone:Click on "Cellular" on the iPhone Settings interface. Find the option of "Wi-Fi Assist" and toggle to turn it off. Android:go to Settings >Cellular, scroll alllll the way to the bottom of that menu, and then turn off Wi-Fi Assist.

Reset Network Settings

There may be some problems with the Phonne system if the WiFi still disconnects when the device is locked. You can solve this problem by resetting the Phone network settings. iPhone:Go to Settings >General >Reset >Reset Network Settings to reset the iPhone's network. You can rest assured that this network reset will not erase the iPhone's data, but some WiFi passwords stored on the phone will be reset. Wait for the system to restore and reboot, then try to connect to the WiFi network to see if the WiFi connection is normal. Android:Open the Settings app on your Android. Scroll to and tap either "General management" or "System," depending on what device you have. ... Tap either "Reset" or "Reset options." Tap the words "Reset network settings." You'll have to confirm that you want to reset your network settings.

Update

There may be some problems with the OS system if the WiFi still disconnects intermittenly. You can solve this problem by updating to latest OS. iPhone:Go to Settings >General, then tap Software Update. Tap Download and Install. To update now, tap Install. If asked, enter your passcode. Android:Open your device's Settings app. Tap Security. Check for an update: To check if a security update is available, tap Security update. To check if a Google Play system update is available, tap Google Play system update. Follow any steps on the screen.
